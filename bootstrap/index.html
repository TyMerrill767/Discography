About the Band

Paul McCartney

Born June 18, 1942, Paul McCartney first played with the Beatles, where he became a legendary figure in rock and roll history. Soon after making Linda Louise Eastman his wife in March 1969, and departing from the Beatles in April 1970, Paul started Wings with Linda, Denny Seawell, and Denny Laine in 1971, continuing the band for ten years through tough times, some critical negativity, and repeated rotation of band members before disbanding it in 1981, being one of the only three members to remain with the band for all ten years. Paul received an MBE in 1965 and was knighted for his services to music in 1997. He continues to make music to this day with many artists such as Dave Grohl, Krist Novoselic, Kanye West, Rihanna, and more.

Linda McCartney

Linda McCartney, née Eastman, born September 24, 1941, was the first wife of Paul McCartney, marrying him in March of 1969. She was first taught to play keyboard by Paul and recorded an album called Ram with him before being inducted into Wings soon after, becoming one of the core three members to remain with the band all throughout its life, along with Paul and Denny Laine. Linda gained wealth and success on her own merit with the start of her line of vegetarian frozen meals in 1991 under the Linda McCartney Foods name. Linda was diagonosed with breast cancer in 1995, and died on April 17, 1998.

Denny Laine

Born October 29, 1944, Denny Laine got into music very early in his life, having his first solo performance at the age of 12 and starting and joining several bands before the age of 20, such as Denny Laine and the Diplomats. Inducted into Wings in 1971 at the band's genesis, Laine became the last of the three core members of the band to stay on until disbandment in 1981. Laine moved to the United States in the 90s, where he continues to perform.

Denny Seiwell

Denny Seiwell, like lane, was introduced to music at a very young age, beginning to learn the drums at only 5 years of age before becoming a professional musician in his mid-teens. Seiwell was inducted into Wings from the very start, but his tenure with the band was not long, departing after a little over two years in 1973. Seiwell has performed with many famous artists such as Art Garfunkel, Janis Joplin, and Billy Joel, and continues to perform even now.

Other Members

Wings had many different band members over its 10-year lifespan, and none but the core three of the McCartneys and Laine remained for very long. The band had three backup guitarists, including Henry McCullogh from early 1972 to early 1973, Jimmy McCulloch from early 1974 to late 1977, and Laurence Juber, who remained from early 1977 until disbandment in 1981. Wings also had four drummers, including the aforementioned founding member Denny Seiwell from their start in 1971 until early 1973, Geoff Britton from mid-1974 to early 1975, Joe English from early 1975 to late 1978, and Steve Holley from late 1978 to the band's disolution three years later.
